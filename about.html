Hi!

I’m Harrison Klostreich, a UI UX Designer based in Asbury Park, New Jersey.

Me, In Three Parts

Music and Design

My introduction to visual design came at a young age as an early teenager. I became enthralled with the music scene and graphic design went hand-in-hand. I was enamored with the blend of unique color schemes, symmetric shapes and outlines, and different blends of artistic ideas that graphic designers of the mid to late 00’s were incorporating into their products. It wasn’t the immediate path I took but it left a lifelong impression.

Usability and Me

As I grew older and eventually finished a degree in music composition, I began to encounter more and more things that bothered me in terms of visual design. “Why is this button here?”, “These colors hurt my eyes,” and “Well that was confusing” were some of the phrases I found myself muttering under my breath as I went about my days not realizing I was making small design critiques.

Communication in Design

The aspect of design that fascinates me the most is that you’re communicating with someone, the user, about how to interact with an app or product. It’s taking a moment and saying “I think this is nice but does it translate to someone else?” being able to communicate with others is a trait I find highly valuable, and the ability to do so is practiced naturally.
